About ​Dallas, TX

Dallas is the largest city in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex, the most populous metropolitan area in Texas and the fourth-largest in the U.S. with a population of 7.5 million people. It is also the seat of Dallas County, with parts extending into Collin, Denton, Kaufman, and Rockwall counties. As of the 2020 census, Dallas had a population of 1,304,379, making it the ninth-largest city in the U.S. and the third-largest in Texas after Houston and San Antonio.

Located in North Texas, Dallas serves as the central hub of the largest metropolitan area in the Southern U.S. and the largest inland metro area in the U.S. without access to the sea. Originally shaped by railroad construction, Dallas grew into an industrial and financial center, with a significant role in cotton, cattle, and oil. The development of major highways and the Dallas Fort Worth International Airport further cemented its status as a transportation hub.

Dallas' economy is diverse, with major industries including defense, financial services, information technology, telecommunications, and transportation. The metroplex is home to 23 Fortune 500 companies, the second-highest number in Texas, with 11 located within the city itself. The region boasts over 41 colleges and universities, the most of any area in Texas. Dallas is known for its diverse population, representing various ethnic and religious backgrounds, and is home to one of the largest LGBT communities in the U.S. In 2018, WalletHub ranked Dallas as the fifth-most diverse city in the country.
